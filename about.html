Class Assignment Summary:

Week 8

Exclusion can manifest in numerous ways, often depending on the context of a particular scenario. Some forms are permanent, reflecting systemic issues within the content creation process. Others may be temporary, arising from specific situations or trends that impact audience engagement-also known as unintended blind spots in content. A product designer can ensure that solutions cater to a broader range of needs, and help foster inclusive design through promoting a future where technology becomes adaptable with human cognition ,while emphasizing the need to consider diversifying potential. Advocates can learn from many perspectives, and evaluate its benefits to others-a methodology that fosters participation and accessibility in a convenient way. By understanding the disabled, creators can brainstorm ideas that allow for a broader audience to connect. My project reuses the idea of catering to one reader at a time, and expands upon the notion of exclusivity by emphasis on detail-oriented characteristics. Tools are designed to meet certain needs of every new or existing novelist to amplify their standard on a cultural scale. Few improvements that need to be addressed are a separate platform for them which maintains additional, special qualities that magnify information into more apparent display, and separate links for the underprivileged, reader group to have a fair chance at literary joy.

Week 9

Chapter 14 explores the integration of evaluation and design, emphasizing its role throughout the design process. Evaluation methods include usability testing, experiments, field studies, and remote evaluations. Controlled methods, like lab-based usability testing, provide high precision, while in-the-wild methods emphasize ecological validity by observing user behavior in natural settings. The chapter discusses formative and summative evaluations, predictive models, heuristic evaluations, and crowdsourcing for broader reach. Key considerations include biases, validity, scope, reliability, and the rights of participants, often protected through informed consent forms. Ethical practices, especially for vulnerable populations, and the impact of ecological validity on results are highlighted. Lastly, combining multiple methods enriches the evaluation process, addressing usability and user experience goals. For an advanced book-filtering system, evaluations could focus on usability and user experience. Formative evaluations would involve heuristic reviews and remote usability testing to refine the interface and ensure filters are intuitive and personalized. Summative evaluations might include in-the-wild studies to observe users’ natural browsing behavior. Crowdsourcing could gather diverse global feedback on filter relevance. Ensuring ecological validity is critical, as users' preferences vary by cultural and personal contexts. Informed consent would protect users’ data and privacy while evaluating their interactions with the system. The combination of these methods ensures the system meets users' unique interests efficiently and effectively.

Week 10

Prototypes serve as powerful tools for organizational change and innovation, as they allow teams to quickly test ideas and gather feedback. In the healthcare context, one team discovered that their initial design—a binder for patient information—was too broad to meet diverse needs. After testing it, they shifted to conversation cards, which facilitated more meaningful interactions between patients and care providers. This change was driven by early feedback, highlighting the value of personal engagement over specific information. Failure, often seen as embarrassing, is embraced within the prototyping process, especially when it leads to small, recoverable errors. The early failure of a “face board” meant to introduce staff to patients revealed that patients preferred learning about familiar providers, not unfamiliar ones. This failure allowed the team to quickly pivot to a photo album showcasing staff’s personal lives, which patients loved. Prototypes also give people permission to explore new behaviors by introducing new tools into familiar environments. In a postpartum healthcare project, a checklist prototype prompted nurses and patients to engage in more effective communication during shift changes, improving patient care. Through iterative prototyping, organizations not only improve their products or services but also encourage a culture of continuous learning and behavioral change, ultimately transforming how they work.

Week 11

Chapter 17 provides an overview of various user interface (UI) types, emphasizing the evolving landscape of interaction design. It delves into voice, wearable, tangible, and brain-computer systems to highlight how they challenge traditional command-based model. While newer technology of gesture and voice recognition have made interaction with digital content more humanized, the chapter denotes limitation expansively, such as the need for extensive training in brain-computer interface. Advancement of sensor technology permits real-time surveillance of users’ cognitive or emotional state where a new possibility for adaptation is opened. Yet, as interfaces become better context-conscious, ethical trouble about privacy and data usage emerge. The chapter stresses that choosing the right display medium depends on the specific context, user need, and task requirement. For instance, healthcare apps may benefit from mobile medium with object recognition, while collaborative decision-making environment might favor shareable devices/services. The reading wraps up by the complexity of design, where factors determine optimal choice. As new interfaces continue to surface, designers must consider both the potential and capacity of these technologies to create worthy user experience. I will apply this knowledge to my project by focusing on how advanced interface designs, such as intuitive mobile apps or AI-powered filters, can enhance user interaction. By understanding how different interface types—like voice, gesture, or AI—can be tailored to specific user needs, I can optimize the filtering system to deliver personalized, seamless book recommendations.I will apply this knowledge to my project to focus on how advanced screen designs, such as intuitive phone apps or AI-powered filters, can enhance user interaction. Comprehension of the types—voice, gesture, or AI—can help tailor to specific user needs, while honing the filter system to deliver personalized, seamless book recommendation.
